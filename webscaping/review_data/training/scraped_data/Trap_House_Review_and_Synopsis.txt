Trap House is directed by Michael Dowse, whose filmography is marked by wild swings that often miss the mark. However, his latest is easily his best since the cult-classic hockey comedy Goon. Working from a script by Gary Scott Thompson, best known for his story credits on the first two chapters of the never-ending Fast & Furious franchise, the movie moves propulsively.

Bautista is too lightweight an actor to carry any film, even one as frivolous as this. However, I did enjoy the gentle-giant DEA agent and dad he plays here, swinging a feather-light hand rather than one wielding a mallet. Especially in the quieter scenes. The film refuses to fall into standard father-son tropes and clich√©s, no matter how ridiculous the situation the kids find themselves in.

I mentioned above that the script does take some risks. For instance, having the core group of teenage characters form a support system together, knowing their parents are undercover drug enforcement officers, is refreshing. Then, presenting plausible scenarios in which they gain privileged intel, equipment, and skill sets feels believable enough not to disrupt your mild enjoyment.

Is Trap House worth watching?
Bobby Cannavale, Kate del Castillo, Dave Bautista, Jack Champion, Sophia Lillis, and Whitney Peak in Trap House (2025) | Image via Aura Entertainment

The script has a mild third-act plot twist that many will see coming. However, there is a second part to this, let‚Äôs call it a plot pivot, that no matter how ludicrous, works within the world Trap House creates. I mean, if the kids of DEA agents can rob for the greater good, then the final twenty-minute plot point, no spoilers, manages to work surprisingly well.

Trap House is worth watching despite some flaws. The film has a kinetic energy that moves briskly, but when it comes to the cover being blown, the script is too flimsy to hide a few noticeable plot holes. The ending also wraps up a little too neatly, leaving an opening for a sequel, which is a personal pet peeve of mine.

However, as a harmless, action-packed one-off with heart, Bautista‚Äôs film is a pleasant surprise for anyone who enjoys this low-rent genre picture.
